Overview

Realistic Air Battles are the most popular air combat mode in War Thunder. They aim to balance realism and accessibility by removing arcade assistance while keeping the game playable without full simulator controls. Player skill, aircraft knowledge, and tactical decision-making are the key factors for success.

Key Features of Realistic Air Battles

  • Realistic flight physics and energy loss.
  • No enemy markers – visual spotting is required.
  • Limited respawns based on lineup and performance.
  • High importance of positioning, altitude, and teamwork.

Aircraft Classes – Realistic Mode Impact

In Realistic Battles, aircraft performance closely reflects real-world limitations. Mistakes such as poor energy management or bad positioning are heavily punished. Each aircraft class fulfills a specific role, and success depends on playing to its strengths.

Aircraft Class Realistic Gameplay Impact Typical Use
Fighters Energy and turn performance matter significantly Air superiority and interception
Heavy Fighters Powerful but vulnerable if mispositioned Boom-and-zoom attacks and bomber hunting
Attack Aircraft High risk due to lack of markers and escorts Ground strikes and ticket reduction
Bombers Require planning, altitude, and team protection Strategic base bombing
Jet Aircraft Energy retention and positioning dominate combat High-speed engagements and missile combat
