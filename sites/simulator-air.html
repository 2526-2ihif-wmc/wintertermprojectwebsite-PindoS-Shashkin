Overview

Simulator Air Battles represent the highest level of realism in War Thunder. This mode removes nearly all interface assistance and places full responsibility on the player. Aircraft handling, navigation, target identification, and situational awareness must be managed manually.

Key Features of Simulator Air Battles

  • Fully realistic flight physics and engine behavior.
  • No HUD, markers, or enemy identification assistance.
  • Cockpit-only or limited external camera views.
  • Severe punishment for mistakes in positioning or awareness.

Aircraft Roles in Simulator Mode

While aircraft classes remain technically the same across all modes, Simulator Battles dramatically change how they are used. Visual identification, manual navigation, and energy management define success far more than raw aircraft statistics.

Aircraft Type Simulator-Specific Challenges Primary Role
Fighters Manual target identification and precise flying Air superiority and interception
Heavy Fighters Limited visibility and poor close-range awareness Boom-and-zoom and bomber interception
Attack Aircraft High vulnerability without situational awareness Ground attack and support missions
Bombers Manual navigation and defensive gunnery Strategic bombing operations
Jet Aircraft Extreme speeds with minimal reaction time High-speed air combat and interception
